VideoElevator : Elevating Video Generation Quality with Versatile Text-to-Image Diffusion Models

Anonymity

Abstract

Text-to-image diffusion models (IDMs) have demonstrated unprecedented capabilities in creating realistic and aesthetic images. However, text-to-video diffusion models (VDMs) still lag far behind in frame quality and text alignment, owing to insufficient quality and quantity of training videos. In this paper, we introduce VideoElevator, a training-free and plug-and-play method, which elevates the performance of VDMs using superior capabilities of IDMs. Different from conventional VDM sampling (i.e., temporal and spatial modeling), VideoElevator explicitly decomposes each sampling step into temporal motion refining and spatial quality elevating. Given noise latent at timestep t, temporal motion refining uses encapsulated VDM to enhance temporal consistency, followed by inverting it to the noise distribution required by IDM. Then, spatial quality elevating harnesses inflated IDM to directly transition to less noisy latent, adding more photo-realistic details. We have conducted experiments in extensive prompts under the combination of various VDMs and IDMs. The results show that VideoElevator not only improves the performance of VDM baselines with foundational IDMs, but also facilitates stylistic video synthesis with personalized IDMs.

VideoElevator for improved T2V generation

Top: Taking text τ as input, conventional VDM performs both temporal and spatial modeling and accumulates low-quality contents throughout sampling chain.
Bottom: VideoElevator explicitly decompose each step into temporal motion refining and spatial quality elevating, where the former encapsulates VDM to enhance temporal consistency and the latter harnesses IDM to provide more faithful details, e.g., dressed in suit. Empirically, applying VDM in several timesteps is enough to ensure temporal consistency.
